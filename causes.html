Causes of Ocean Pollution

From industrial runoff to unchecked plastic waste, the causes of ocean pollution are manifold. Discover the primary contributors to this global issue.

Plastic Waste: Millions of tons of plastic enter the oceans each year. These plastics break down into microplastics which are ingested by marine animals, affecting the food chain.

Industrial Runoff: Factories and industrial plants often discharge untreated chemicals and waste into the oceans, leading to toxin accumulation.

Oil Spills: Large oil spills from tanker ships are disastrous for marine ecosystems, covering marine life in toxic oil and depleting oxygen levels.

Agricultural Runoff: Pesticides, fertilizers, and other chemicals used in farming flow into the oceans through rivers, leading to 'dead zones' where nothing can survive.

Marine Transport: Shipping activities result in the release of toxic substances, garbage disposal, and noise pollution that disrupt marine life.
